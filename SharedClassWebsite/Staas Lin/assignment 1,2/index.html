Jack and Sons: Weaving Traditions with Love

In the heart of Willowbrook, Jack and Sons has woven a legacy spanning generations. Since 1912, our family has crafted exquisite baskets, blending tradition with innovation. From harvest baskets during the Great Depression to eco-friendly designs today, each piece carries stories of community, resilience, and craftsmanship. Our workshop, nestled amidst rolling hills and whispering willows, buzzes with the rhythmic sound of weaving. Here, locals gather to share laughter, secrets, and a love for our craft.

Ethan and Oliver, Jack’s sons, expanded our business, experimenting with materials like reeds, rushes, and even recycled newspaper. Our baskets graced weddings, picnics, and cozy hearths, woven with care and passion. During World War II, they carried rations and hope. Post-war, they found their way into stylish boutiques and elegant homes.

Today, Jack’s great-grandson, Jack, leads the way. He blends age-old techniques with modern sensibilities. Our commitment to sustainability extends beyond products; we plant a tree for every basket sold. Explore our website to discover our handcrafted baskets and learn more about our heritage. Unveil the artistry. Feel the warmth.

Welcome to Jack and Sons, a place where passion meets craftsmanship.
